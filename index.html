The First Space Odyssey

The real human space odyssey began in the 1950s, during the Space Race between the Soviet Union and the United States. In 1957, the Soviets launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, starting the Space Age. A few months later, they sent Laika, the first living creature to orbit Earth.

In 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space, orbiting Earth aboard Vostok 1. This marked a major milestone in human history. The United States then created the Apollo program, leading to the first Moon landing in 1969, when Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface.
